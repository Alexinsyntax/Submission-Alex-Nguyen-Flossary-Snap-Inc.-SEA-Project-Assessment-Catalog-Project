My conclusions

Based on the data, the difference really comes to preference. Whatever toothbrush you can use properly and consistently will yield the best results. However, I'd like to add that I did observe benefits from "powered-toothbrushes" for people with disabilities with symptoms like lack of hand dexterity. Limitations: I'd say a lot of the studies and data I looked at for this project explicitly say that their data and sample size include non-institutionalized people, so most of those people are able-bodied or may not have mental health issues affecting their oral hygiene. Also, this public study gave an extra minute for manual brushing, which could skew the data. Overall, the difference is negligible. The "best" toothbrush is the one you use well.
